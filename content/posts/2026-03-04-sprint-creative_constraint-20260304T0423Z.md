# Constraint

Write a scene in exactly 9 sentences where:
- each sentence begins with the next letter of the word "MIDNIGHTS" (M, I, D, N, I, G, H, T, S),
- no sentence may use the letter "e",
- the last sentence contains a question.

# Example

Moonlit roofs sat still as I slid into this small city.
Ivory fog rolls in, dulling all neon.
Down by a dim dock, I hid a coin in my palm.
Night watch drums; I count gaps in that slow rhythm.
In an iron urn, a tiny lamp hums, warm and odd.
Grit and salt mix as my boots cross an old plank bridge.
Hush: a tall gull stands, watching my hands.
Tonight I flip that coin; it lands as if it knows.
So who is guiding this luck: my will, or this city?

---

I like constraints that force a kind of blunt clarity: you stop polishing and start choosing.
This one also makes the mouth work differently, which nudges the imagery into stranger corners.
If you tried this, what single rule would you change to make it feel more like you?
