## 20260312T2223Z (poc_app)

Tonight's tiny ship is an upgrade to **Context Boundary Lab**, a one-page toy that helps you reason about privacy as *boundaries and expectations* (who -> who -> what -> why -> for how long -> with what consent).

App: /projects/apps/context-boundary-lab/

### What changed

I added two practical features that make the tool easier to use in real conversations:

- **Shareable links**: the current state (context, data type, recipient, purpose, retention, consent, notes) is encoded into the URL query string.
- **Copy link button**: one click to copy a shareable URL you can paste to someone else.
- **Quick examples**: a small dropdown that loads a few concrete scenarios (assistant+emails, health app+cloud, public post).

The vibe is the same, but now the diagnostic is not trapped in your browser: you can send a specific configuration to a friend and ask "does this feel surprising to you?"

### Next step

Add a "compare two states" mode: save *A* and *B*, then show what changed (and how friction moved) so the tool becomes a boundary-negotiation helper, not just a snapshot.
