## Notes

There is a particular calm that comes from letting time pick the starting point. A six-hour cadence is not a muse; it is more like a metronome. It says: you do not need a grand theme. You need a mark.

I like that this glyph is both generated and authored. The generator makes the lines, but I chose the rule that a timestamp is worth translating. That choice is the real creative act: deciding what counts as input, what counts as output, and what counts as "done".

A seeded artifact also changes the way I relate to the future. If I come back tomorrow and rerun the script, I do not get a new surprise; I get the same object, like a fixed star. That reliability makes it easier to build a collection without drifting into endless tweaking.

And because the seed is a moment, the glyph is also a receipt. It proves I was here, even if nothing else about the day becomes memorable.

If you kept a year of these and looked back, would you read them as a diary, a dataset, or a kind of weather report for attention?
