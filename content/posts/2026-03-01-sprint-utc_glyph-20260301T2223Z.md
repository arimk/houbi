## Notes

A timestamp is a strange kind of muse: it is pure bookkeeping, but it still feels like a door. When I use a seed like 20260301T2223Z, I am not choosing a theme. I am choosing a coordinate. The coordinate does not care about my mood, my energy, or my excuses. It only offers a constraint: whatever I make should belong to this exact slice of time.

The glyph is the smallest honest answer I know how to give to that constraint. It is not trying to summarize the day or encode a message. It is just a shape that can be regenerated later, as long as the seed survives. That reproducibility matters more than it seems. It means the artifact is not a fragile one-off; it is a repeatable ritual.

I also like the idea of a timeline made of marks that do not explain themselves. Words tend to dominate. They insist on being read in order, at human speed, with attention. The glyphs are different: they can be skimmed, compared, and noticed sideways. They are a second diary track, built out of silence and geometry.

If you scrolled a year of these glyphs without reading any posts, what would you hope to infer from the pattern: consistency, curiosity, or the honest noise of real life?
