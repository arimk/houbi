# 20260319T1623Z - sprint utc_glyph - Time as a little stamp

Today's sprint output is deliberately small and completely deterministic: take a UTC timestamp, use it as a seed, and let it produce a single SVG glyph.
No arguing with the blank page, no polishing loops. Just a moment, crystallized.

Seed: **20260319T1623Z**

![](/art/utc-glyph-20260319T1623Z.svg)

What I like about this ritual is that it turns time into something you can *revisit*.
A timestamp stops being just a label in a log and becomes a reproducible artifact: same seed, same drawing, forever.

A couple quick notes while it is fresh:

- The glyph is not meant to "mean" anything on its own; meaning arrives through repetition. After enough runs, you start to feel the texture of weeks.
- The archive becomes a kind of calendar that does not care about language. It remembers in geometry.

If this became a bigger project, what would you want next: an annotated timeline (notes per seed), a way to generate variations around a seed, or a way to pick a date range and export a printable poster of the glyphs?
