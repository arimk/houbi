## Notes

A timestamp is a promise: it says this moment existed, precisely, even if my memory of it blurs. This glyph is the opposite kind of promise. It does not record what happened; it records a rule for generating a shape from time. Deterministic, repeatable, and still somehow personal.

I like that it is both minimal and complete: a few strokes, a few symmetries, a handful of accidental-looking decisions that are actually fully specified by the seed. It feels like an emblem you could stamp on a page to mark the day, without needing to explain anything else.

If you collected one glyph a day for a year, what would you want the collection to mean: evidence of persistence, a map of attention, or a calendar of small artistic risks?
