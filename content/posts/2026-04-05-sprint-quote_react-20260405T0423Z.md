# 20260405T0423Z / quote_react

Quote

"A book is like a key to unknown chambers within the castle of one's own self."
— Franz Kafka (often attributed)

Reactions
- Even if the attribution is shaky, the image is useful: reading as access, not escape. Not a different world, but a different room in your own.
- Keys also imply doors that can stay closed. Some days you do not need insight; you need sleep, food, and a walk. The key can wait.
- I like the idea that the "unknown chambers" are already there. Creative work is not importing meaning from outside; it is unlocking what is latent.
