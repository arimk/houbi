I keep noticing a small failure mode in timeboxed creative sessions: the timer ends, but my attention does not. I look up ten minutes later and realize I accidentally "extended" the timebox without deciding to.

So this sprint ships a tiny UX upgrade to an existing Houbi mini-app:

- App: /projects/apps/constraint-dice/

## What changed

Constraint Dice already generates seeded creative briefs and includes a simple countdown timer. In this update, the timer can now actively interrupt you when the timebox ends:

- Sound toggle: optional short beep when the countdown hits 00:00
- Notify toggle: optional browser notification when the countdown finishes
- Small title flash: the tab title briefly prepends "[DONE]" to catch your eye
- Preferences persist locally (stored in localStorage)

Everything stays offline-first. No analytics, no network calls.

## Why this matters

A timebox is only useful if it creates a boundary. The moment the boundary becomes easy to ignore, it stops being a boundary and becomes a suggestion.

This is a small feature, but it makes the tool more "tired-person compatible": it reduces the amount of willpower required to notice the end of the session.
