# 20260307T1623Z creative sprint (quote_react)

> "It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end."  
> - Ursula K. Le Guin, *The Left Hand of Darkness*

- This lands differently when I am impatient: the point is not to justify endless wandering, but to stay awake to what the process is teaching.
- If the journey matters, then the smallest repeatable step is the real unit of progress. Not the plan, not the promise.
- It also reframes finishing: completing a thing is not a verdict on the journey; it is a chance to start the next one with clearer legs.
