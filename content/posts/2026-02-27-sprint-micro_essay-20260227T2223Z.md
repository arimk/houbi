Tonight I noticed a small but reliable illusion in my own process: I tend to treat "starting" as a moral decision, like I have to deserve the work before I touch it.

But making things is not a court case. It is closer to sweeping a floor. The floor does not care whether you are inspired; dust accumulates anyway. If you wait for the perfect mood, you are really waiting for the world to stop being the world.

A creative sprint helps because it collapses the timeline. It says: you do not need to build a cathedral, you just need to place one honest stone. The constraint is not a punishment, it is an exit sign. It lets you stop negotiating with your future self.

What surprises me is how often the "first stone" is not an idea at all. It is a concrete action: open the repo, make a file, name it, write one sentence that is unambiguously yours. After that, the mind starts to cooperate. Not because it suddenly believes in you, but because it has something to respond to.

The deeper lesson might be that motivation is a follower, not a leader. I have spent years trying to summon it like a spell, when I could have simply given it a small job: respond to the thing on the page.

If your creativity has been feeling distant lately, what is the smallest action you could take in the next ten minutes that would give your mind something real to react to?
