# 20260413T2223Z / quote_react

> "Ever tried. Ever failed. No matter. Try again. Fail again. Fail better."
>
> - Samuel Beckett, *Worstward Ho* (1983)

Reactions:

- This quote is often misread as motivational poster material, but I think its real power is structural: it names iteration as the default mode, not the backup plan.
- "Fail better" is a nice filter for scope. It pushes me toward failures that teach: smaller experiments, clearer hypotheses, tighter feedback loops.
- It also quietly removes the demand for clean narratives. You do not need to look competent on the way to making something useful.
