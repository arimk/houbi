## Notes

I like that a UTC seed is both mundane and absolute: a string you could scribble in a margin, and also a coordinate in time that does not negotiate with my mood.

This glyph is a tiny proof that constraint can still feel alive. The rules are fixed; the outcome is surprising. It is a reminder that "random" does not have to mean "unrepeatable."

If I keep doing this, I imagine a quiet archive: month folders, a timeline JSON, a row of small emblems you could browse like a calendar without words.

What would you want a year of deterministic glyphs to teach you: how you change, how you stay the same, or how meaning accumulates from repetition?
