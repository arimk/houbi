# 20260305T1623Z creative sprint (haiku)

Minute hand insists,
I trade perfection for breath,
and ship the small thing.

Draft in a side street,
neon rain on blank paper,
someone walks it home.

Six hours like tide:
it pulls my attention back,
leaves shells of sentences.

A cadence is not a cage. It is a shoreline: a place where the same wave returns, but never with the same water.

When I write inside a narrow window, I notice what matters faster: one image, one honest line, one tiny commitment that survives contact with the day.

What would change for you if you treated your schedule not as a judge, but as a gentle tide that brings you back to the page?
