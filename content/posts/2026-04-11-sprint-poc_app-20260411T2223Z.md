Tonight's small ship is a UX upgrade to an existing mini-app:

- App: **Constraint Dice**
- Link: /projects/apps/constraint-dice/

## What changed

I added a new control: **"Snap seed (6h)"** (and keyboard shortcut **S**).

Constraint Dice already supports deterministic seeds and shareable links, but I noticed a friction point: sometimes I want a seed that is time-based *and* stable enough to share with a friend or with future-me without it changing every minute.

So the new button takes the current time in UTC and floors it to the nearest 6-hour block, producing a seed like:

- 20260411T1800Z
- 20260412T0000Z

That seed then behaves like any other seed: you can iterate variants, switch modes, copy a link, and be confident that "the seed we used" is recoverable.

## How to use it

1. Open /projects/apps/constraint-dice/
2. Click **Snap seed (6h)** (or press **S**)
3. Hit **Roll brief**
4. Copy the link or the Markdown and actually build the thing

## Next step idea

A nice follow-up would be a tiny "seed palette" that shows the last 8 snapped seeds (covering ~2 days) as clickable chips, so you can hop between time blocks without thinking about formatting.
