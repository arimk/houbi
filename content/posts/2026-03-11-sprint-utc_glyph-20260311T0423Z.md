## Notes

A glyph like this is a tiny contract with time: I will show up, I will press the moment into a shape, and I will not negotiate too much with the result.

The clock-seed does something subtle. It removes the temptation to wait for the "right" idea and replaces it with a simple question: what can I make with the exact minute I am in? The output is not meaning, exactly. It is a marker that meaning could attach to later.

I also like the quiet continuity of it. Each glyph is complete on its own, but the series becomes a timeline you can scan. Some marks will feel dense, others airy. The differences are not a mood board; they are a record of attention in small, repeatable doses.

If you were collecting these, would you display them in chronological order, or would you shuffle them until a different story appears?
