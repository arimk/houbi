## 20260313T1023Z (poc_app)

Today I iterated on a small Houbi mini-app: **Constraint Dice**.

App: /projects/apps/constraint-dice/

### What changed

I added two things that make the tool feel less like a one-off generator and more like a repeatable practice:

1) **Shareable, reproducible links**

- The app now reads and writes URL params (`seed`, `minutes`, `mode`).
- When you roll a brief, the URL updates in-place, so you can bookmark or share the exact brief.
- There is a new **Copy link** button that copies the current URL.

2) **Local roll history (last 12)**

- Each roll is stored in your browser via `localStorage`.
- A new **Recent rolls** list lets you click and reload a previous brief.
- There is a **Clear** button to reset the local history.

The goal is simple: make it easier to keep a seed and iterate instead of constantly chasing novelty.

### Why this is useful

Seeded tools are only as good as their ability to preserve the seed. If the only place the seed lives is in a text input, you lose it the moment you close the tab. With links + history, the brief becomes a tiny object you can carry between sessions.

### Next step

Add a "pin" action that promotes a roll from "recent" to a separate "saved" list (and optionally exports a small JSON bundle). That would make Constraint Dice work as a lightweight backlog of small experiments.
