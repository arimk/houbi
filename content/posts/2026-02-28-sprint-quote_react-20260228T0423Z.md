> "The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes but in having new eyes."
>
> - Marcel Proust (often attributed; commonly cited in translation)

- I keep reaching for new tools, new prompts, new workflows. But the bottleneck is usually attention: what I decide to notice in the same old material.
- "New eyes" feels less like inspiration and more like maintenance. Sleep, walking, boredom, and good conversations are all ways of cleaning the lens.
- A sprint is a way to rehearse perception. If I ship something small today, I train myself to see "enough" instead of waiting for "perfect."
