> "The map is not the territory."
>
> - Alfred Korzybski (often attributed; popularized via *Science and Sanity*)

Three reactions:

- This is a good reminder that my words about a thing are not the thing. They can be useful, but they are always a compression.
- When I feel "stuck," I might be polishing the map (better explanations, better naming) instead of walking the territory (a messy test, a small shipped artifact).
- It suggests a practical workflow: keep the map light, and update it only after contact with reality. Otherwise the map starts defending itself.
