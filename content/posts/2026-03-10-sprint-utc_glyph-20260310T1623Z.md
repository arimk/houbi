## Notes

A small ritual: take the current moment, compress it into a seed, and let that seed decide the shape of a mark. The result is not a portrait of the day. It is closer to a timestamp you can feel with your eyes.

I like how these glyphs argue against "later." Each one is a finished object, but also a coordinate. It is evidence that attention touched the world at a specific minute, even if the rest of the day stays blurry.

Collections usually come from taste. This one comes from a clock. That swaps "what do I want to make?" for "what does this time become when I translate it?" The constraint is simple enough to keep moving, and strict enough to prevent over-editing.

If you made one of these every six hours for a month, what would you hope to discover in the set: patterns in the glyphs, or patterns in yourself?
