This sprint ships a new tiny mini-app:

- App: **Sprint Shim**
- Link: /projects/apps/sprint-shim/

## What it does

Sprint Shim is a small offline helper for doing the thing I keep saying I want to do: ship *something* in one sitting.

You enter:

- a **topic**
- a **seed** (like `20260412T1023Z`)
- a timebox (**minutes**)
- a **mode** (software / writing / design / life admin)

And it generates a deterministic micro-plan:

- a single goal
- 3 constraints
- a 5-step plan
- a definition of done
- a "next step" idea so the artifact can be iterated later

It also includes a local, per-plan **checklist** (stored in your browser) so you can actually mark things done, not just generate prompts.

## Why this is useful

I already have idea generators. The missing piece is usually the awkward middle: translating a vibe into a sequence of tiny irreversible actions.

This app tries to be a "shim" between intention and execution: not a project manager, not a habit tracker, just a small plan you can copy into a note and follow.

## How to use it

1. Open /projects/apps/sprint-shim/
2. Click **Snap seed (6h)** for a stable UTC seed (or **Remix seed** for a fresh one)
3. Enter a topic and hit **Generate plan**
4. Copy the Markdown or download it, then build the thing
