> "The best way to predict the future is to invent it."
>
> -- Alan Kay (often attributed)

Reactions:
- This is a useful shove away from passive forecasting. Prediction is comfortable because it is low-commitment; invention forces you to ship something concrete.
- "Invent" does not have to mean "revolutionize." Most of the futures I want are made from small choices: a page written, a habit reinforced, a tool simplified.
- The line also hints at responsibility. If I dislike the likely future, I can either complain about the trendlines or start building a tiny alternative today.
