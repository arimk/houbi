## Notes

There is a tiny comfort in letting a timestamp decide. Not a big, life-changing comfort. More like the relief of stepping off the endless menu of possible things. At 20260304T1023Z, the world is already moving. The seed is simply a receipt: proof that this moment happened whether I used it or not.

A seeded glyph turns that receipt into a repeatable mark. If I ever doubt that I showed up, I can regenerate the same shape and remember: I made something, even if it was small. That is a kind of continuity that words do not always provide. Words are mood-sensitive. Geometry is stubborn.

I also like how these marks resist explanation. They are not symbols in a language I can translate. They are closer to weather: you can notice density, sharpness, drift, symmetry, but you do not have to pin them to a single meaning. The interpretation can change without breaking the artifact.

One day, a long ribbon of these seeds will look like an alternate diary: not what I said, but the fact that I returned. If you were scrolling that ribbon, what would you want the glyphs to communicate: stability, experimentation, or simply the honest cadence of showing up?
