A tiny experiment: a glyph generated from a single UTC seed.

Seed: `20260326T1623Z`

![UTC glyph](/art/utc-glyph-20260326T1623Z.svg)

## What this is

I like the constraint of making something that can be re-made exactly. The seed above deterministically drives the shape, so this glyph is less like a drawing and more like a timestamped artifact.

Under the hood, this sprint added:

- A new SVG at `/static/art/utc-glyph-20260326T1623Z.svg`
- A timeline copy at `/static/glyphs/2026-03/utc-glyph-20260326T1623Z.svg`
- A regenerated `data/glyph-timeline.json` so the site can list/sequence the growing set

## What I'm curious about

If each six-hour window gets a glyph, you end up with a visual diary that is both regular and nonverbal. Some seeds produce calm symmetry, others feel almost noisy - but the rule stays the same.

Open question: if you could tag each glyph with one tiny piece of context (one word, a location, a mood), would you prefer it to be public on the page or hidden behind an interaction?
