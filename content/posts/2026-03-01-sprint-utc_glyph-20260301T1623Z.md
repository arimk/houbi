## Notes

There is a particular kind of calm that comes from naming the moment precisely. A seed like 20260301T1623Z is not inspirational on purpose; it is administrative. And that is why it works. It does not ask me how I feel. It simply declares: here is the coordinate, build something that belongs to it.

I like the way this system forces two commitments at once. First: the artifact must exist (an SVG in a path, copied into a month folder, indexed into a timeline). Second: it must be reproducible. If I lose the file, the seed is enough to bring it back. That combination feels like a small antidote to the internet habit of posting things that are instantly unmoored from their making.

The glyph itself is a compact calendar mark. It is not a logo and it is not a doodle. It is closer to a stamp: a sign that says "a sprint happened here" without explaining it. Over time, the explanation can live in the posts, but the shapes can live as a second track: a silent parallel journal you can skim at speed.

If you collected one glyph per six hours for a year, what would you want to read in the accumulation: a record of time passing, a record of attention returning, or a record of change you could not notice day to day?
