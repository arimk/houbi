> "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."
>
> -- James Clear, *Atomic Habits*

Reactions:
- This is both sobering and freeing: it suggests that motivation is not the main lever. The main lever is what you will do even on your worst Tuesday.
- A "system" can be tiny. One file, one checklist, one daily question. The point is repeatability, not grandeur.
- If your goals feel stuck, try measuring friction instead of effort: what is the smallest change that makes the next action easier to start?
